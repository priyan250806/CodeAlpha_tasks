What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, and personal data.

⚡ Did You Know? Over 3.4 billion phishing emails are sent every single day. It is the #1 cause of data breaches worldwide, responsible for 36% of all breaches in 2023.
📧

Email Phishing

Fraudulent emails that appear to come from banks, government agencies, or popular services like PayPal, Google, or Amazon.

📱

Smishing (SMS)

Phishing via text messages — fake delivery notifications, OTP requests, or prize alerts that trick you into clicking malicious links.

📞

Vishing (Voice)

Phone call scams where attackers impersonate bank officials, tech support, or government officers to extract sensitive information verbally.

🎯

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks customized for a specific individual using personal details gathered from social media or data leaks.

🐋

Whaling

Spear phishing aimed at high-value targets — CEOs, CFOs, or senior executives — to authorize large wire transfers or expose company secrets.

🌐

Pharming

Redirects users from legitimate websites to fake ones by corrupting DNS settings — even if you type the correct URL, you land on the attacker's site.

⚠️ Real World Impact In 2022, a phishing attack on Uber resulted in a complete network breach. An employee was tricked into providing MFA codes via WhatsApp, granting attackers full access to internal systems.

Recognizing Phishing Emails

Here is a real-world phishing email simulation. Every red flag is annotated — study each one carefully.

FROM: security-alert@paypa1-verify.com ⚠ FAKE DOMAIN
TO: you@gmail.com
SUBJECT: ⚠️ URGENT: Your PayPal account has been suspended! ⚠ URGENCY
DATE: Sun, 31 May 2026 03:14:22 +0000 ⚠ ODD HOUR

Dear Valued Customer, ⚠ NO NAME


We have detected suspicious activity on your PayPal account. Your account has been temporarily suspended due to multiple failed login attempts.


To restore access and avoid permanent suspension, you must verify your identity within 24 HOURS. ⚠ FAKE DEADLINE


Click below to verify your account immediately:


👉 http://paypa1-secure-verify.suspicious-domain.ru/login ⚠ FAKE URL


Failure to verify will result in permanent account closure and fund forfeiture. ⚠ THREAT


Regards,
PayPal Security Team ⚠ NO CONTACT INFO

🔴

Fake Domain

paypa1-verify.com — the letter "l" replaced with "1". Legitimate PayPal emails only come from @paypal.com

⏰

Artificial Urgency

"24 HOURS", "permanent closure", "immediate action" — attackers create panic to bypass your rational thinking.

👤

Generic Greeting

"Dear Valued Customer" — real companies address you by name. Generic greetings are a strong phishing indicator.

🔗

Suspicious URL

Hover over links before clicking. The domain ends in .ru and contains random words — never a legitimate company URL.

✅ Pro Tip — Always Hover Before You Click Before clicking any link in an email, hover your mouse over it and look at the actual URL shown in the bottom-left of your browser. If it doesn't match the sender's official domain, do not click.

Recognizing Fake Websites

Attackers create convincing clones of real websites. Here's how to spot them before entering any credentials.

⚠ PHISHING SITE SIMULATION — DO NOT INTERACT

🔓 http://secure-paypa1-login.verify-account.ru/signin

🔵 PayPal — Log In

© 1999-2026 PayPal, Inc. All rights reserved.

CHECK LEGITIMATE SITE ✅ PHISHING SITE ❌
URL paypal.com paypa1-login.verify-account.ru
HTTPS 🔒 Secure 🔓 Not Secure
Domain Age Registered years ago Registered days ago
Contact Info Full address, phone None or fake
Grammar Professional Errors, odd wording
🔍 How to Verify a Website Use whois.domaintools.com to check when a domain was registered. Phishing sites are usually registered within the last few days. Also check virustotal.com — paste any suspicious URL and it scans it against 70+ security engines instantly.

Social Engineering Tactics

Attackers exploit human psychology, not technical vulnerabilities. Understanding their tactics is your best defense.

😱

Fear & Urgency

Creates panic to make you act without thinking. Phrases like "your account will be deleted", "legal action pending", or "virus detected" trigger fight-or-flight responses.

"Your bank account has been frozen due to suspicious activity. Call us NOW at +1-800-FAKE to avoid permanent closure."
🎁

Greed & Reward

Promises of prizes, lottery wins, job offers, or unclaimed money. If something sounds too good to be true — it is.

"Congratulations! You've been selected to receive ₹50,000 from the Government of India COVID Relief Fund. Click here to claim."
🤝

Authority & Trust

Impersonates figures of authority — CEOs, police, tax officials, IT departments — to make victims comply without questioning.

"This is the CBI Cyber Crime Division. We have detected illegal activity linked to your Aadhaar number. Press 1 to speak with an officer immediately."
💕

Romance & Friendship

Builds emotional relationships over weeks or months before asking for money, gift cards, or personal information. Common on dating apps and social media.

"I'm a US Army soldier stationed abroad. I've fallen for you. I just need you to send me $500 in Google Play cards to get emergency leave approved."
🆘

Pretexting

Creates a fabricated scenario to extract information — pretending to be IT support, HR, or a new colleague who needs your credentials to help you.

"Hi, this is Raj from IT. We're doing a security audit and need you to confirm your password to verify your account hasn't been compromised."
⚠️ The Golden Rule No legitimate bank, government agency, or tech company will EVER ask for your password, OTP, PIN, or CVV over email, SMS, or phone call — under any circumstances.
// Module 05
Best Practices & Protection

Follow these practices to protect yourself and your organization from phishing attacks.

01

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even if attackers steal your password, MFA blocks them. Use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy) rather than SMS-based OTP — SIM swapping can bypass SMS MFA.

02

Verify Before You Click

Hover over all links before clicking. If you receive an urgent email from your bank, don't click the link — open a new tab and navigate to the website manually.

03

Use a Password Manager

Password managers auto-fill credentials only on the correct domain. If you land on a fake site, the manager won't auto-fill — an instant phishing detector.

04

Keep Software Updated

Browser updates include phishing site blacklists. Enable auto-updates for your OS, browser, and antivirus to get the latest protection automatically.

05

Report Suspicious Emails

Don't just delete — report phishing emails. In Gmail, use "Report Phishing". In Outlook, use "Report Message". This helps protect everyone in your organization.

06

Check Email Headers

Real emails pass SPF, DKIM, and DMARC checks. In Gmail, click the three dots → "Show original" to inspect headers and verify the email's true origin.

07

Educate Your Team / Family

The weakest link is always human. Share this training with people around you. A single untrained person can compromise an entire organization.

✅ Quick Reference Checklist
  • Verify the sender's email domain before responding
  • Hover over links — check the actual URL destination
  • Look for HTTPS 🔒 and verify the exact domain spelling
  • Never share OTP, password, or CVV over call/SMS/email
  • Use MFA on all important accounts
  • When in doubt — call the company directly using their official number
  • Check virustotal.com for any suspicious links or files
